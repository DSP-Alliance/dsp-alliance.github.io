FIL+: 1x Multiplier

Below we provide a detailed analysis of the free choice multiplier position as the optimal choice in FIP Discussion #774, Motivation for a Scheduled Reduction in the Quality Multiplier of Fil+ Deals and Example Timelines. Here, we outline its significant advantages for network macroeconomics, security, and the comprehensive health of the system.

Simplicity and Clarity

Free choice multipliers offer a simple and clear solution to the complexities of network economics. By allowing service providers (SPs) to independently choose their pledge multiplier from a range of 1x to 10x, we eliminate the need for complex calculations and adjustments. This approach simplifies the process and makes it more transparent for all participants. It also eliminates the potential for incorrect inferences, such as the assumption that all pledge becomes 10x, which can lead to confusion and misinterpretation. The simplicity of this approach also reduces the potential for errors and misunderstandings, which can have significant impacts on network performance and security.

Fairness and Equality

The free choice multipliers approach also promotes fairness and equality among network participants. By allowing SPs to choose their own multiplier, we ensure that all participants have the same opportunities and are not disadvantaged by arbitrary or unfair rules. This approach also prevents the potential for negative impacts on returns, network RBP, token supply, and consensus security, which could occur if multipliers were immediately halted for new sectors. By providing a level playing field, we ensure that all participants can compete fairly and have equal opportunities to succeed. This approach also respects the goals of participants to be a network for actual data storage, rather than simply a platform for financial speculation.

Long-Term Sustainability

The free choice multipliers model offers a sustainable solution for the long-term operation of the network. It provides a mechanism that is not only flexible but also adaptable to the changing needs and dynamics of the network. This model allows for the independent choice of pledge from 1x to 10x, which can be adjusted according to the specific requirements of the Storage Providers (SPs).
This flexibility is crucial for maintaining the health and stability of the network in the long run. It allows SPs to adapt to changing market conditions and client demands, without being constrained by a rigid multiplier system. This adaptability is key to ensuring the network's resilience and longevity.
Moreover, the free choice multipliers model does not have any apparent near-term undesirable effects at a network level. It directly addresses the primary motivating problems of the original proposal and appears to have no detrimental effects on network macroeconomics or security. This ensures the long-term sustainability of the network, as it can continue to function effectively without compromising on its core objectives and principles.

Prevention of Corruption and Abuse

The free choice multipliers model also plays a significant role in preventing corruption and abuse within the network. By allowing SPs to independently choose their pledge, it eliminates the possibility of manipulation or exploitation of the multiplier system. In the original proposal, there were concerns that halting multipliers for any new sectors could have negative impacts on returns, network RBP, token supply, and consensus security. However, the free multipliers proposal dispenses with these concerns, as it does not involve any abrupt changes that could potentially disrupt the network.
Furthermore, the free choice multipliers model does not rely on human governance, which further reduces the risk of corruption and abuse. It operates on a transparent and objective mechanism, which ensures fairness and accountability in the network. It provides the flexibility and adaptability needed for long-term operation, while also ensuring fairness and preventing abuse.

Sovereignty and Freedom

The free choice multipliers model is a testament to the principle of sovereignty and freedom in network operations. It allows Storage Providers (SPs) to independently choose their pledge from a range of 1x to 10x, providing them with the autonomy to make decisions that best suit their business models and strategic objectives. This flexibility is a significant advantage, especially for SPs focused on client-paid deals, as they can enjoy lower pledge if they choose to do so.
This model also eliminates the need for human governance in Quality Adjusted Power (QAP), thereby reducing the potential for human error and bias. It also simplifies the process by removing unnecessary complexities associated with the governance of QAP. The free choice multipliers model, therefore, not only enhances the efficiency of network operations but also empowers SPs by giving them the freedom to make independent decisions.

Avoiding the Infinite Loop

One of the potential pitfalls of a fixed multiplier system is the possibility of creating an infinite loop, where the multiplier keeps increasing without any upper limit. This could lead to a situation where the pledge becomes excessively high, thereby creating an unsustainable situation for SPs. The free choice multipliers model effectively addresses this issue by allowing SPs to choose their multiplier, thereby preventing the occurrence of an infinite loop.
Moreover, the free choice multipliers model does not have any apparent near-term undesirable effects at a network level. It does not negatively impact network RBP, token supply, or consensus security. On the contrary, it directly addresses the primary motivating problems of the original proposal and appears to have no detrimental effects on network macroeconomics or security.
The free choice multipliers model is a robust and flexible system that not only simplifies network operations but also empowers SPs by giving them the freedom to make independent decisions. It effectively addresses the potential pitfalls of a fixed multiplier system and ensures the long-term sustainability of the network.


The Right Choice

In conclusion, the free choice of multipliers is a powerful tool that can help us achieve our key goals. It is a solution that is simple, fair, sustainable, and resistant to corruption and abuse. Most importantly, it respects the sovereignty and freedom of the SPs, and avoids the pitfalls of an infinite loop. It is a change worth exploring and implementing.
